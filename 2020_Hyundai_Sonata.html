PRIMARY METHOD

STEERING WHEEL CONTROLS
1

START YOUR ENGINE

Get in the driver's seat. Insert key, fire up the engine. Make sure your vehicle is in PARK position with all doors closed.

2

ACCESS MENU SYSTEM

Locate the MODE button on your steering wheel (usually on the right side). Hit this button to cycle through to the USER SETTINGS menu.

3

FIND SERVICE INTERVAL

Use the directional buttons to scroll down until you find "SERVICE INTERVAL". Press the OK button firmly to select it.

4

RESET THE SYSTEM

When you see "Service Required" on the display, press and HOLD the OK button for at least 1 full second. Don't tap it - HOLD IT DOWN.

5

VERIFY RESET

The service interval will reset to default settings. You'll see a confirmation on your dash. Job done. Reset complete.

IMPORTANT

The service interval feature must be ACTIVATED first. Access the INFORMATION menu by pressing the MODE button, then press OK to enter the menu. Use the directional pad to turn on SERVICE INTERVAL mode and set your preferred interval.

ALTERNATE METHOD

INFOTAINMENT SYSTEM
1

ENTER SETUP MENU

With engine running, access your infotainment screen's main menu. Select "SETUP" option.

2

VEHICLE SETTINGS

In the Setup menu, locate and tap "VEHICLE" to access vehicle-specific settings.

3

FIND CONVENIENCE

With newer software updates, scroll down to find the "CONVENIENCE" option in the Vehicle menu.

4

SERVICE INTERVAL

Under Convenience, locate and select the "SERVICE INTERVAL" option with a firm press.

5

RESET COUNTER

Follow the on-screen prompts to reset your service interval. Confirm when prompted. Done.

SOFTWARE UPDATE INFO

If you've recently updated your Hyundai's software, menu locations may have changed. Older versions had Service Interval under SETUP > VEHICLE > CLUSTER. Newer versions moved it to SETUP > VEHICLE > CONVENIENCE.

PRO MECHANIC TIP

Always reset your oil life indicator IMMEDIATELY after changing your oil. Failure to reset can cause your maintenance schedule to be thrown off, giving you inaccurate readings about when service is actually due. It's the mark of a true gearhead to keep maintenance tracking precise.

OIL TYPE MATTERS

Your 2020 Sonata demands SAE 5W-30 full synthetic oil. Don't cheap out with conventional oil. Synthetic costs more but gives superior engine protection, especially in extreme temperatures. Performance vehicles deserve performance oil.

CHANGE FREQUENCY

Factory specs say change every 7,500 miles under normal conditions. But if you're driving hard, towing, or in extreme weather, cut that to 3,750 miles. Real pros know that more frequent changes mean longer engine life.

FILTER QUALITY

Don't just change the oil - replace the filter with OEM or better. A high-quality filter traps more contaminants and maintains proper oil pressure. Skip the bargain filters if you want your engine to last.

TROUBLESHOOTING

CAN'T FIND SERVICE INTERVAL OPTION

If the Service Interval option is nowhere to be found, you're likely dealing with a software update that moved things around. Try these fixes:

1. Check under Vehicle > Convenience instead of Vehicle > Cluster

2. Some software versions may have eliminated the option from the infotainment system. In this case, use the steering wheel controls method.

3. If all else fails, disconnect the battery for 5 minutes to force a system reset (note: this will reset ALL settings).

RESET PROCEDURE NOT WORKING

If holding the OK button doesn't reset the service light:

1. Make sure you're holding it for at least 1 FULL second - not just tapping it

2. Try turning the car completely off, wait 30 seconds, then restart and try again

3. Ensure the Service Interval feature is actually activated in your vehicle settings

4. Some vehicles require pressing and holding multiple buttons simultaneously - try holding OK + Back buttons together

SERVICE LIGHT RETURNS IMMEDIATELY

If your service light comes back on right after resetting:

1. Your oil level may actually be low - check with the dipstick

2. There could be a sensor malfunction - have the oil pressure sensor checked

3. The oil quality sensor might be detecting contaminated oil - make sure you've actually changed the oil with fresh supply

4. There may be another maintenance issue triggering the light - consider a diagnostic scan
