Key Differences

Compare regular and heavy-duty suspension characteristics

Regular Suspension
  • Leaf Springs: 2-4 thin leaves, lighter gauge steel
  • Shocks: Standard diameter (1-2 inches), single tube design
  • Frame: Lighter construction, smaller cross-members
  • Ride Height: Lower ground clearance (6-8 inches)
  • Axle: Smaller diameter axle housing
  • Payload: 1,000-3,500 lbs typical capacity
Heavy-Duty Suspension
  • Leaf Springs: 6-12+ thick leaves, heavy gauge steel
  • Shocks: Large diameter (2.5+ inches), often dual-tube
  • Frame: Reinforced construction, larger cross-members
  • Ride Height: Higher ground clearance (8-12+ inches)
  • Axle: Thick, heavy-duty axle housing
  • Payload: 3,500+ lbs capacity, up to 7,000+ lbs
