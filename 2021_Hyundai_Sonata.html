OIL LIFE RESET GUIDE

2021 HYUNDAI SONATA

Model: SONATA | Year: 2021 | Engine Options: 1.6T / 2.5L / Hybrid

RESET YOUR OIL LIFE INDICATOR

Keeping track of your Hyundai Sonata's oil life is essential for maintaining optimal engine performance and longevity. After changing your oil, resetting the service indicator ensures accurate maintenance tracking. Follow one of these methods to reset your 2021 Hyundai Sonata's oil life indicator.

USER SETTINGS METHOD
PRIMARY METHOD

  • PREPARE YOUR VEHICLE

    Ensure your 2021 Sonata is parked safely with the parking brake engaged. Close all doors and put the transmission in Park position.

  • START THE ENGINE

    Start your Sonata's engine and let the dashboard initialize fully. Wait until all initial startup notifications have cleared.

  • ACCESS USER SETTINGS

    Press the MODE button on your steering wheel to navigate to the USER SETTINGS menu on your instrument cluster display.

  • FIND SERVICE INTERVAL

    Use the arrow buttons or scroll wheel to navigate down to SERVICE INTERVAL, then press the OK button to select it.

  • RESET THE SERVICE INTERVAL

    Within the SERVICE INTERVAL menu, select RESET and confirm when prompted. The service interval will reset to 100% or your default setting.

IMPORTANT NOTE

If you cannot find the SERVICE INTERVAL option in the menu, it may be located under SETUP > VEHICLE > CONVENIENCE in some updated software versions. Menu locations can vary slightly between different Sonata trim levels.

TRIP/OIL LIFE METHOD
ALTERNATIVE

  • TURN IGNITION ON

    Turn the key to the "On" position without starting the engine. For push-button start vehicles, press the START button once without pressing the brake pedal.

  • ACCESS DISPLAY OPTIONS

    Locate the "Mode" or "Trip" button on your dashboard. This is typically found on the right side of the steering wheel or on the instrument panel.

  • NAVIGATE TO OIL LIFE SCREEN

    Press the Mode/Trip button repeatedly until you see the "Oil Life" screen displayed on your instrument cluster. You may need to cycle through other displays like trip odometer and fuel economy first.

  • RESET THE OIL LIFE

    Once the Oil Life screen is displayed, press and hold the OK button (or Reset button) for approximately 5 seconds until the oil life indicator resets.

  • CONFIRM THE RESET

    The display should confirm the reset by showing "Oil Life Reset" or "100%" or similar message. Turn the ignition to the Off position to complete the process.

PRO TIP

If the reset doesn't work on the first attempt, try turning the ignition completely off, wait 10 seconds, and then repeat the steps. Some models may require you to press and release the button several times rather than holding it down.

DASHBOARD CONTROLS

The steering wheel controls (highlighted) are used to navigate the instrument cluster menus. The MODE button and OK button are used to access and reset the service interval.

TROUBLESHOOTING

SERVICE INTERVAL MENU NOT FOUND

Some 2021 Sonata models, especially following software updates, may have the Service Interval option relocated. Try looking under:

  • SETUP > VEHICLE > CONVENIENCE
  • SETUP > VEHICLE > CLUSTER
  • VEHICLE SETTINGS > MAINTENANCE

If you still cannot find the option, check your owner's manual or contact your Hyundai dealer. They may need to reset it using their diagnostic equipment.

RESET BUTTON NOT RESPONDING

If pressing and holding the OK/Reset button doesn't reset the oil life indicator, try these steps:

  1. Make sure you're holding the button long enough (5-10 seconds)
  2. Try a sequence of quick presses rather than holding
  3. Turn the ignition completely off, wait 30 seconds, then try again
  4. Disconnect the vehicle's battery for 5 minutes (this will reset all electronic systems, not just the oil life)

Note: Disconnecting the battery will also reset other settings such as clock, radio presets, and seat memory.

OIL LIFE INDICATOR RETURNS AFTER RESET

If the service reminder returns shortly after resetting, this could indicate:

  • The reset procedure wasn't completed correctly
  • There might be other maintenance issues causing the system to trigger
  • A possible sensor malfunction

If the indicator persists after multiple reset attempts, it's recommended to have your vehicle inspected by a Hyundai service technician. They can use diagnostic tools to identify and resolve any underlying issues.

NO OIL LIFE PERCENTAGE DISPLAY

Some Hyundai models, including certain 2021 Sonata trims, may not display an actual oil life percentage. Instead, they use a simple service interval reminder based on mileage or time.

If your vehicle doesn't show an oil percentage, focus on resetting the service interval reminder rather than looking specifically for an "oil life" display. The SERVICE INTERVAL option in your settings menu is what you need to reset.

OIL SPECIFICATIONS

🛢️
RECOMMENDED OIL TYPE
  • 2.5L Engine: SAE 5W-30 Full Synthetic
  • 1.6T Engine: SAE 5W-30 Full Synthetic
  • Hybrid: SAE 5W-30 Full Synthetic
  • Always use oil meeting Hyundai specifications
  • API Certified (look for the API starburst symbol)
  • ILSAC GF-6 Certified recommended
📏
OIL CAPACITY
  • 2.5L Engine: 5.8 quarts (5.5 liters) with filter
  • 1.6T Engine: 4.8 quarts (4.5 liters) with filter
  • Hybrid: 4.2 quarts (4.0 liters) with filter
  • Always verify with dipstick after filling
  • Over-filling can damage engine components
  • Consult owner's manual for exact specifications
⏱️
CHANGE INTERVALS
  • Normal Conditions: Every 7,500 miles / 12 months
  • Severe Conditions: Every 3,750 miles / 6 months
  • Hybrid: Every 7,500 miles / 12 months
  • Follow service reminder for optimal timing
  • Severe conditions include frequent short trips, extreme temperatures, stop-and-go traffic, or towing
  • Regular oil changes extend engine life
